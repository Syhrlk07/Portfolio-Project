## Summary

Adam Hart shares his experience after extensively testing several AI-powered video editing tools, including Descript, EddieAI, AutoCut, and Choppity. While acknowledging that AI is improving, he argues that current tools still struggle with the most important aspect of professional editing: storytelling. Instead of replacing editors, AI is currently most valuable for technical and repetitive production tasks.

---

## Key Insights

- Storytelling remains a uniquely human skill that current AI editing tools cannot reliably replicate.
- AI provides the greatest value when automating repetitive technical workflows rather than creative decision-making.
- Audio enhancement, green screen cleanup, and repetitive editing tasks are practical use cases where AI already improves productivity.
- Overpromising AI capabilities creates unrealistic expectations and reduces trust among professional creators.

---

## Why This Matters

For B2B companies producing YouTube content, editing is not simply about removing mistakes or generating transcripts. Effective videos rely on narrative structure, pacing, and audience engagement—areas that still require experienced human editors. AI should therefore be viewed as a productivity tool rather than a replacement for strategic video editing.

---

## Possible Applications

- Use AI tools to automate repetitive editing workflows while keeping creative storytelling human-led.
- Prioritize narrative quality over editing speed when producing educational B2B videos.
- Combine AI-assisted production with professional editorial review before publishing.
- Adopt AI selectively for technical improvements instead of relying on full automation.
