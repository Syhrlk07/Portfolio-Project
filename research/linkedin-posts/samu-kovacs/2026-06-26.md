## Summary

Samu Kovács explains that success on B2B YouTube should not be measured by millions of views. Instead, the primary objective is attracting the right Ideal Customer Profile (ICP) and converting viewers into qualified business leads. He shares a real client example where a YouTube video with only 67 views generated a highly qualified lead, demonstrating that relevance is more valuable than reach.

---

## Key Insights

- B2B YouTube success is measured by qualified leads, not total views.
- A small number of highly targeted viewers can generate significant business opportunities.
- B2C content focuses on virality and audience growth, while B2B content focuses on attracting the right ICP.
- High-quality content targeting the correct audience can produce strong ROI even with low view counts.
- Measuring business outcomes is more important than tracking vanity metrics.

---

## Why This Matters

This post highlights one of the biggest differences between B2B and B2C YouTube strategies. B2B SaaS companies should prioritize creating content that attracts decision-makers rather than chasing viral performance. A video with relatively few views can still generate substantial business value if it reaches the right audience.

---

## Possible Applications

- Measure YouTube performance using qualified leads instead of view count.
- Create videos designed for a clearly defined Ideal Customer Profile (ICP).
- Focus on solving niche industry problems rather than maximizing viral reach.
- Track conversions and business outcomes alongside YouTube analytics.
