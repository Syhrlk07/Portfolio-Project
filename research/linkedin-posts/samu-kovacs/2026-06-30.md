## Summary

Samu Kovács argues that podcasts and YouTube videos should not be treated as the same type of content. He explains that many B2B companies fail on YouTube because they simply upload podcast recordings without designing them specifically for YouTube audiences. Instead, successful channels plan their ideas, research, packaging, and scripting before recording, creating what he calls "YouTube-first content."

---

## Key Insights

- Podcasts and YouTube videos serve different purposes and should not follow the same content strategy.
- Uploading podcast recordings directly to YouTube rarely generates significant views or qualified leads.
- High-performing B2B YouTube channels invest heavily in planning, research, packaging, and scripting before production.
- A YouTube-first approach focuses on creating content specifically optimized for YouTube's audience and discovery system.
- Educational, standalone videos are generally more discoverable than repurposed podcast episodes.

---

## Why This Matters

This post highlights a common mistake made by many B2B companies: treating YouTube as merely a distribution channel for podcasts. Instead, Samu emphasizes designing content specifically for YouTube from the beginning. For B2B SaaS companies, this strategy can improve discoverability, attract new buyers, and generate qualified leads through educational videos tailored to the platform.

---

## Possible Applications

- Design YouTube content independently instead of repurposing podcast recordings.
- Develop video ideas based on audience search intent and educational value.
- Create a structured pre-production workflow that includes research, scripting, and thumbnail/title planning.
- Use YouTube as a customer acquisition channel rather than simply a content archive.
- Prioritize educational videos that answer audience questions and solve real business problems.
