## Summary

Samu Kovács explains that effective YouTube titles generally fall into two categories: search-optimized titles and algorithm-optimized titles. He argues that creators should choose their title strategy based on the size and behavior of their target audience rather than following a single universal approach. For smaller B2B niches, search-focused titles generate consistent long-term traffic, while larger markets benefit more from browse-optimized titles designed to attract viewers through YouTube recommendations.

---

## Key Insights

- YouTube titles should match the audience size and content discovery behavior.
- Search-optimized titles are effective for niche B2B markets with limited audience demand.
- Algorithm-optimized titles help reach broader audiences through YouTube's recommendation system.
- Understanding the difference between search intent and browse intent is essential for long-term channel growth.

---

## Why This Matters

This post directly relates to YouTube content strategy for B2B SaaS. It emphasizes that title optimization is not only about improving click-through rates but also about aligning content with how the target audience discovers videos. For B2B SaaS companies operating in niche industries, choosing search-driven titles can generate consistent evergreen traffic and qualified leads over time.

---

## Possible Applications

- Use keyword-focused titles for educational videos targeting specific B2B search queries.
- Develop evergreen YouTube content that ranks in search results over the long term.
- Reserve browse-optimized titles for broader awareness campaigns or larger target markets.
- Analyze audience behavior before deciding whether to prioritize search or recommendation traffic.
