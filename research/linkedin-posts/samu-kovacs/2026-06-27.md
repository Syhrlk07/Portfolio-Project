## Summary

Samu Kovács explains that most creators fail on YouTube because they assume viewers already trust them. In reality, every new viewer is a stranger who decides within the first 10–30 seconds whether to continue watching. He argues that creators must immediately establish credibility before teaching anything, giving viewers a clear reason to trust their expertise.

---

## Key Insights

- Every YouTube viewer starts with zero trust.
- The first 10–30 seconds determine whether viewers continue watching.
- Creators should establish authority before delivering educational content.
- Demonstrating experience or proven results increases viewer confidence.
- Founders often avoid talking about their achievements because they fear appearing arrogant, but credibility is necessary for audience retention.

---

## Why This Matters

For B2B SaaS companies, YouTube is built on trust. Potential customers are unlikely to learn from a company or founder if they are not convinced of their expertise. This post highlights the importance of opening videos with credibility signals, such as business achievements, client results, years of experience, or measurable outcomes, to improve audience retention and authority.

---

## Possible Applications

- Begin videos with a credibility statement or proven achievement.
- Use the first 30 seconds to explain why the audience should trust the speaker.
- Include client results, case studies, or business metrics early in the video.
- Design video introductions that establish authority before teaching.
